# Brand Guidelines

## Product Positioning

Main App is a premium white-label local business website builder platform. It helps local service businesses launch polished websites, collect leads, and eventually run light operations from one owner-friendly dashboard.

The product should feel:

- practical
- premium but not flashy
- local-business fluent
- trustworthy
- fast to understand
- built for owners who have work to do

## Audience

Primary users:

- local service business owners
- solo operators growing into teams
- small home-service, appointment, food, fitness, and retail businesses
- agencies or operators who may white-label the platform later

They care about:

- getting leads
- looking legitimate
- saving time
- avoiding tool sprawl
- not being trapped by agencies or generic website builders

## Brand Voice

Use plain, confident language.

Prefer:

- "Get a website that brings in real leads."
- "Built around your business type."
- "Works even before AI is connected."
- "Review before publishing."

Avoid:

- hype-heavy SaaS jargon
- legal/financial certainty
- claims of guaranteed revenue
- implying AI is required
- promising final pricing or payment features before scoped

## Visual Direction

The platform brand should be clean and modern, with enough warmth to feel approachable to small businesses.

Recommended feel:

- neutral base
- strong typography
- restrained accents
- high-contrast CTAs
- real business examples
- clear dashboard surfaces

Avoid:

- one-note purple/blue gradients
- excessive decorative blobs
- generic startup hero art
- pages that look like templates rather than business tools

## Client Brand Intake Questions

Ask these before creating or tuning a client website:

- What is the business name?
- What industry or service category best describes it?
- What city, region, or service area should the site target?
- What services should be featured first?
- What is the main action visitors should take: call, request quote, book, view menu, visit location, or contact?
- Does the business have a logo?
- Does the business have brand colors?
- Does the business have real photos?
- What tone should the site use: premium, friendly, urgent, family-owned, technical, playful, luxury, rugged?
- What proof exists: reviews, years in business, licenses, before/after photos, guarantees, local awards?
- What should never be said on the site?
- Are prices fixed, estimate-only, or not public?
- What competitors or example websites does the owner like or dislike?

## Copy Rules

- Local context matters more than generic polish.
- Use the client's real services and service area.
- Do not invent licenses, guarantees, awards, reviews, or years in business.
- Do not hard-code final pricing.
- For AI-assisted copy, always allow fallback blueprint copy.
