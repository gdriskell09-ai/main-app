## Purpose

This document captures the compliance-first principles that must govern all future payment, business operations, and legal features on the Main App platform. Reference this before scoping any phase that touches money, customer data, contracts, or communications.

## What Is NOT Built Yet (and must stay that way until explicitly scoped)

- Live payment collection (Stripe, Square, or any processor)
- Raw card number storage of any kind
- Contract generation or e-signature
- Tax automation or tax filing
- SMS/email campaign sending
- Audit log system
- Role-based team access

---

## Payment Collection Principles

When payment collection is built, it must follow these rules without exception:

| Rule | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Use a PCI-compliant hosted checkout or payment element (Stripe, Square) | We are never PCI-compliant enough to handle raw card data ourselves |
| Never store raw card numbers, CVVs, or full PANs in our database | PCI DSS violation risk; legal liability |
| Store only processor tokens (Stripe `payment_method` ID, etc.) | Safe to store; useless to an attacker |
| Confirm payment status server-side via webhook, never client-side | Client can be spoofed |
| Implement idempotency keys on all payment API calls | Prevent double-charges on network retries |
| Log all payment events (created, captured, refunded, disputed) | Required for dispute resolution and audit |

**Approved processors to evaluate:** Stripe (preferred for developer experience), Square (preferred for in-person + retail), Authorize.net (alternative for certain industries)

## Business Operations Features (Future)

### Invoicing & Receipts
- PDF-ready invoice templates tied to customer + job record
- Payment links embedded in invoices (hosted by processor, not us)
- Automatic receipt on payment capture
- Support for deposits (partial payment), full payment, and balance-due states

### Refunds & Cancellations
- Refund flow must call processor refund API — never manually adjust records
- Business owner sets cancellation policy in settings
- Policy text shown to customer at checkout / on invoice
- Chargeback/dispute notes field on payment record (manual tracking at first)

### Sales Tax
- Placeholder settings field: "Tax rate (%)" in business settings
- Display-only — no automatic calculation or remittance at first
- Disclaimer in settings: "Tax calculation and remittance is your responsibility. Consult your accountant."
- Future: Stripe Tax or TaxJar integration when explicitly scoped

### Payment Status Tracking
- Statuses: `unpaid` | `partially_paid` | `paid` | `refunded` | `disputed` | `cancelled`
- Tied to invoice records in Supabase
- Admin dashboard view filtered by status

## Legal & Compliance Features (Future)

### Pages Required Before Launch of Paid Features
- `/terms` — Terms of Service (must be reviewed by attorney before enabling payments)
- `/privacy` — Privacy Policy (must cover data collection, storage, and sharing)
- `/cookies` — Cookie/analytics consent notice if any tracking pixel or analytics are added

### Customer Communications Consent
- SMS opt-in: explicit checkbox on quote form and contact form — "I agree to receive SMS updates about my service"
- Email marketing opt-in: separate from transactional email consent
- Opt-out: every SMS and marketing email must include unsubscribe/opt-out mechanism
- Store consent timestamp + source in customer record

### Data Storage & Security
- Customer PII (name, email, phone, address) stored in Supabase with RLS
- No plaintext passwords — Supabase Auth handles all authentication
- Do not log PII in server console output or error messages
- Implement data retention policy before collecting payment data

### Role-Based Access (Team Members)
- Roles: `owner` | `admin` | `viewer` | `rep` (door-to-door rep)
- `owner` — full access including billing and settings
- `admin` — full ops access, no billing
- `viewer` — read-only CRM and job data
- `rep` — canvass map and lead submission only
- Enforce at Supabase RLS level (row-level policies per role), not just UI

### Audit Logs
- Log these events: payment captured, payment refunded, quote approved/rejected, invoice sent, contract sent, team member role changed, account password reset, API key generated
- Store: `user_id`, `action`, `resource_type`, `resource_id`, `timestamp`, `ip_address` (hashed)
- Retention: minimum 2 years

## AI-Generated Content Disclaimer

All AI-generated copy, contracts, templates, and business documents produced by this platform carry this disclaimer (must appear in UI wherever AI content is generated):

> "This content was generated by an AI assistant and is provided for informational purposes only. It is not legal advice, financial advice, or a substitute for professional consultation. Review all documents with your own attorney, accountant, or qualified professional before using them in your business."

This disclaimer must appear:
- In the AI Generator section of the admin dashboard ✓ (add if not present)
- On any generated contract or proposal document
- In the terms of service

## Implementation Order (When Scoped)

1. **Terms of Service + Privacy Policy pages** — needed before any paid tier goes live
2. **Invoice and payment status tracking** (no payment collection yet — just records)
3. **Stripe payment elements** — hosted checkout for invoice payment links
4. **Role-based team access** — after multi-user need is confirmed
5. **SMS/email consent flows** — before any campaign feature launches
6. **Audit logs** — before any sensitive financial action is live
7. **Sales tax settings** — placeholder first, full integration later with accountant guidance
8. **Chargeback/dispute tracking** — alongside full Stripe integration

## What Business Owners Must Be Told

Before enabling payment collection for any business on this platform, we surface this notice in onboarding:

> **Your responsibility:** Main App provides the tools to collect payments and manage your business. You are responsible for:
> - Setting up and verifying your payment processor account
> - Understanding your local sales tax obligations
> - Reviewing your cancellation and refund policy with a professional
> - Ensuring your use of customer data complies with applicable laws (GDPR, CCPA, CAN-SPAM, TCPA)
> - Consulting an attorney for any contracts or legal documents you send to customers
