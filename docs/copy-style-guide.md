# Copy Style Guide

## Main App Voice

Clear, grounded, and owner-friendly.

Write for busy local business owners who want practical results, not software jargon.

## Good Copy

- "Launch a site that matches your business type."
- "Start with blueprint content, improve it with AI later."
- "Review before publishing."
- "Built for quotes, calls, bookings, and local trust."

## Avoid

- guaranteed lead/revenue claims
- final pricing promises
- legal or tax certainty
- "AI will do everything" claims
- generic filler like "revolutionary platform"
- invented reviews, licenses, awards, or business history

## Website Copy Rules

- Mention the real service area.
- Use the client's actual services.
- Make the first CTA match the business: call, quote, book, menu, contact, visit.
- Keep headlines specific to the industry.
- Use proof only when provided.
- Keep pricing estimate-only unless explicitly approved.

## AI Copy Rules

- AI content must be reviewed by the owner before publishing.
- AI generation must be button-triggered.
- If AI fails or is unavailable, use blueprint/fallback content.
- Never require an AI key for the base website builder.

## Client Intake Copy Prompts

- What do customers usually ask before hiring you?
- What jobs or services do you want more of?
- What areas do you serve most?
- What makes your business safer or easier to choose?
- What should your website never promise?
- What proof can we show?
